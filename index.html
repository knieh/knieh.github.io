idea8

Collaborators : Yashna Bowen, Fairy Bui, Annie Chen, Kathy Hoang, Alex Kim, Jamie Moon, Kenny Nieh

Role : As the project manager of the group, I was in charge of maintaining grounded design rationale and user advocacy for the project, mentoring groupmates new to UX research and design, and keeping the group on track with our weekly goals and deliverables.

Idea8 is a personal tool that enables you to maximize your ideation iteratively and collaboratively. It draws inspiration from Google's design sprint's exercise: Crazy Eights, where you are given a piece of paper split into 8 panels and you must ideate and sketch out 8 ideas within 5 minutes. Idea8 creates a virtual version of crazy eights and enables you to control for situational variability, changing the number of panels and the amount of time allotted per panel, and also gives you the option to ideate and get feedback from peers, anywhere, anytime.

Throughout the 10 weeks allotted for our project, four major components were constantly evaluated and honed: user research, iterative prototyping, user testing, and sound design rationale. The cycle begins with conducting user research, creating features or prototypes based on our users' needs and data, testing those features through mock-ups, and revisiting our design rationale as a group to reflect on our design decisions and maintain strong supporting evidence. What was key to this process is that each step was iterative in order to strengthen and broaden our research and design rationale and build better, more relevant versions of idea8.
